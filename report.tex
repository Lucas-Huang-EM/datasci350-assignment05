This report explores a subset of World Development Indicators (WDI)
across countries in 2022.\\
We focus on the relationships among economic development (GDP per
capita), health outcomes (life expectancy), and other social indicators
such as unemployment, inflation, and inequality.

\section{Relationship between life expectancy and GDP per
capita}\label{relationship-between-life-expectancy-and-gdp-per-capita}

We expect countries with higher GDP per capita to generally have higher
life expectancy, though the relationship may flatten at high income
levels.

\begin{figure}

\centering{

\pandocbounded{\includegraphics[keepaspectratio]{report_files/figure-pdf/fig-life-gdp-output-1.pdf}}

}

\caption{\label{fig-life-gdp}Life expectancy vs GDP per capita (log
scale).}

\end{figure}%

As shown in Figure~\ref{fig-life-gdp}, life expectancy tends to increase
as GDP per capita increases.

\section{Inequality and life
expectancy}\label{inequality-and-life-expectancy}

Next we examine whether countries with higher income inequality tend to
have lower life expectancy.

\begin{figure}

\centering{

\pandocbounded{\includegraphics[keepaspectratio]{report_files/figure-pdf/fig-ineq-life-output-1.pdf}}

}

\caption{\label{fig-ineq-life}Life expectancy vs income inequality.}

\end{figure}%

Figure Figure~\ref{fig-ineq-life} suggests that there isn't a
significant association between life expectancy and income inequality.
It may be slightly negative but more tests are needed and may not be
significant.

\section{Conclusion}\label{conclusion}

In 2022, GDP per capita is positively associated with life expectancy
(Figure~\ref{fig-life-gdp}).\\
Other indicators such as inequality and unemployment may also relate to
health outcomes, although the strength and direction of these
relationships vary across countries.
